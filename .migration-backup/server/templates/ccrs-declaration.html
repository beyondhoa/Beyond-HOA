ARTICLE IDefinitions
"Association"
Beyond HOA at Lakeview Commons Homeowners Association, Inc., a nonprofit corporation.
"Board"
The Board of Directors of the Association, as constituted from time to time.
"Common Area"
All real property owned by the Association for the common use and enjoyment of Owners, including but not limited to: entry features, roadways, parking areas, landscaping, the pool, fitness center, and clubhouse.
"Lot"
Any numbered parcel shown on the Subdivision Plat, intended for development and use as a single residential unit.
"Owner"
The record owner of fee simple title to any Lot, excluding persons or entities holding an interest merely as security.
"Occupant"
Any person other than an Owner who legally occupies a Lot, including tenants, family members, and authorized guests.
"Development"
Lakeview Commons, as shown on the Subdivision Plat recorded in Plat Book 142, Pages 18–26.
"Declaration"
This Declaration of Covenants, Conditions, and Restrictions, as amended from time to time.
ARTICLE IIProperty Rights
§ 2.01 – Owner's Rights to Common Area
Every Owner shall have a right and easement of enjoyment in and to the Common Area, subject to: (a) the right of the Association to suspend the enjoyment rights of an Owner who is delinquent in assessments; (b) the right of the Association to establish reasonable rules for use of Common Areas; and (c) the right of the Association to dedicate or transfer Common Area to any public agency.
§ 2.02 – Delegation of Use
Any Owner may delegate their right of enjoyment of the Common Area to members of their household or to tenants. Owners who rent their Lots are responsible for ensuring their tenants are aware of and comply with all Association rules and these CC&Rs.
§ 2.03 – Lot Use Restrictions
Each Lot in Lakeview Commons is restricted to residential use only. The following uses are expressly prohibited:
  • Commercial or business operations conducted from a Lot that generate regular customer traffic to the unit
  • Storage of hazardous materials, chemicals, or substances in quantities exceeding household use
  • Raising or keeping of livestock, poultry, or exotic animals
  • Any activity that creates a nuisance, hazard, or unreasonable interference with neighbors' quiet enjoyment
  • Short-term rentals (fewer than 90 consecutive days) of any kind
ARTICLE IIIMaintenance Obligations
§ 3.01 – Owner Maintenance
Each Owner shall maintain their Lot and all improvements thereon, including the exterior of the structure, in good condition and repair, in a manner consistent with the character and quality of the Development. The Association shall have the authority to perform maintenance that an Owner has failed to complete following due notice and to assess the cost against the Owner's Lot as a special assessment.
§ 3.02 – Association Maintenance
The Association shall maintain all Common Areas, including landscaping, roads, parking areas, exterior lighting, the pool and aquatics facilities, the fitness center, and the clubhouse in good condition and repair. The cost of such maintenance shall be funded through regular assessments levied against all Owners.
§ 3.03 – Damage and Waste
No Owner, Occupant, or guest may damage, deface, or waste any portion of the Common Area or common structures. Any Owner shall be liable for damage to Common Areas caused by the Owner, Occupants, or guests of that Owner's Lot, and the Association may assess such costs against the Owner as a special assessment.
ARTICLE IVArchitectural Control
§ 4.01 – ARC Authority
No exterior alteration, addition, structure, fence, or other improvement shall be commenced, erected, or maintained upon any Lot, nor shall any exterior addition or alteration thereto be made, until the plans and specifications have been submitted to and approved in writing by the Architectural Review Committee (ARC) established by the Board.
§ 4.02 – Standards
The ARC shall approve proposed improvements only where they conform to the Architectural Review Guidelines and are of a quality consistent with the overall character of the Development. The ARC shall not be required to grant approval in cases of design incompatibility even if technically compliant with written guidelines.
§ 4.03 – Deemed Approval
If the ARC fails to respond to a complete application within thirty (30) days, the application shall be deemed conditionally approved, subject to compliance with all applicable codes, laws, and Association standards. The Owner assumes all risk in the event of non-compliance discovered upon final inspection.
ARTICLE VAssessments & Liens
§ 5.01 – Covenant to Pay Assessments
Each Owner, by acceptance of a deed to a Lot, covenants and agrees to pay to the Association: (a) regular annual assessments levied for the purpose of funding Association operations and reserves; (b) special assessments for capital improvements or extraordinary expenses; and (c) individual assessments for damage caused by the Owner or the Owner's guests.
§ 5.02 – Priority of Lien
Unpaid assessments shall constitute a lien on the respective Lot, superior to all other liens except: (a) ad valorem tax liens; (b) all sums unpaid on a first mortgage of record; and (c) any sums unpaid on any mortgage or deed of trust made in good faith and for value recorded prior to the lien of the assessment. The lien shall be effective from the due date of the assessment.
§ 5.03 – Subordination of Lien
The lien of assessments shall be subordinate to the lien of any first mortgage. The sale or transfer of any Lot shall not affect the assessment lien; however, the sale of a Lot pursuant to foreclosure of a first mortgage shall extinguish the lien as to assessments that became due prior to such sale.
ARTICLE VIEnforcement & Remedies
§ 6.01 – Enforcement Authority
The Association, by and through the Board, may enforce any provision of this Declaration, the Bylaws, or the Rules and Regulations by: (a) imposing fines and penalties; (b) suspending an Owner's use privileges; (c) pursuing specific performance or injunctive relief in a court of competent jurisdiction; or (d) exercising the Association's lien rights.
§ 6.02 – Self-Help Remedy
If an Owner fails to maintain their Lot as required, the Association may, following reasonable notice and opportunity to cure, enter upon the Lot and perform necessary maintenance or corrective work. All costs incurred shall be assessed as an individual assessment against the Lot and shall constitute a lien thereon.
§ 6.03 – Remedies Cumulative
Each remedy provided in this Declaration is cumulative and not exclusive. The Association's election of one remedy shall not preclude the pursuit of any other available remedy. The prevailing party in any enforcement action shall be entitled to recover reasonable attorney's fees and court costs.
ARTICLE VIIDuration & Amendment
§ 7.01 – Duration
The covenants and restrictions of this Declaration shall run with and bind the land and shall be enforceable by the Association or any Owner for an initial term of thirty (30) years from the date of recordation. Thereafter, they shall automatically renew for successive ten-year periods unless terminated by a vote of seventy-five percent (75%) of the total membership.
§ 7.02 – Amendment
This Declaration may be amended by the affirmative vote of seventy-five percent (75%) of the total Owners at a duly noticed meeting at which a quorum is present. Any amendment must be recorded in the Office of the County Recorder within sixty (60) days of adoption to be effective. No amendment may eliminate the requirement to fund reserves or eliminate any Owner's right to the use of Common Areas without unanimous consent.
