ARTICLE IName, Location, and Purposes
§ 1.01 – Name
The name of this organization is Beyond HOA at Lakeview Commons Homeowners Association, Inc. (the "Association"), a nonprofit corporation organized under the laws of the State.
§ 1.02 – Principal Office
The principal office of the Association is located at 8400 Lakeview Commons Boulevard, Suite 100. The Board of Directors may change the principal office location upon proper notice to all Members.
§ 1.03 – Purposes
The purposes of the Association are to:
  • Manage, maintain, and improve the Common Areas and shared amenities within Lakeview Commons.
  • Enforce the CC&Rs, Rules and Regulations, and these Bylaws for the benefit of all Members.
  • Establish and collect assessments sufficient to fund the Association's operations and reserves.
  • Promote the health, safety, and welfare of all residents within the community.
  • Represent the collective interests of Members in dealings with governmental agencies and third parties.
ARTICLE IIMembership and Voting Rights
§ 2.01 – Membership
Every owner of a unit within Lakeview Commons is a Member of the Association. Membership is automatic upon conveyance of title and terminates upon transfer of ownership. Membership is appurtenant to unit ownership and may not be separated therefrom.
§ 2.02 – Voting Rights
Each Member shall be entitled to one (1) vote per unit owned. In the event a unit is owned by more than one person, the co-owners shall designate one person to cast the vote for that unit. Joint owners who fail to designate a voter shall forfeit the vote for that election cycle.
§ 2.03 – Good Standing Requirement
Members more than thirty (30) days past due on any assessment shall be considered not in good standing and shall not be entitled to vote or hold office until all delinquent amounts, including interest and late fees, are paid in full.
§ 2.04 – Proxies
Members may vote by proxy. All proxies must be in writing, signed by the Member, and delivered to the Secretary before the meeting. Proxies shall be valid only for the specific meeting and adjournments thereof for which they are submitted. No person may hold more than three (3) proxies.
ARTICLE IIIBoard of Directors
§ 3.01 – Number and Qualifications
The Association shall be managed by a Board of Directors consisting of five (5) directors. Each director must be a Member of the Association in good standing. No more than one (1) member of the same household may serve simultaneously on the Board.
§ 3.02 – Terms of Office
Directors shall serve staggered two-year terms. Three (3) directors are elected in odd-numbered years; two (2) directors are elected in even-numbered years. No director shall serve more than three (3) consecutive full terms without a one-year break.
§ 3.03 – Powers of the Board
The Board shall have the following powers and duties:
  • Adopt and amend rules and regulations not inconsistent with the CC&Rs or these Bylaws.
  • Establish and collect regular and special assessments.
  • Engage contractors, employees, and management companies for Association operations.
  • Enforce the governing documents through fines, suspension of privileges, and legal action.
  • Invest and expend Association funds in accordance with fiduciary duties.
  • Borrow money and incur obligations on behalf of the Association.
§ 3.04 – Quorum and Voting
A majority of the entire Board of Directors shall constitute a quorum. Board action requires the affirmative vote of a majority of directors present at a meeting at which a quorum is present. In the event of a tie, the President shall cast a deciding vote.
§ 3.05 – Removal of Directors
Any director may be removed from office, with or without cause, by the affirmative vote of a majority of the total membership at a duly noticed special or annual meeting. A director who is absent from three (3) or more consecutive regular Board meetings without an excused absence shall be deemed to have resigned.
§ 3.06 – Vacancies
Vacancies on the Board shall be filled by the remaining directors for the remainder of the unexpired term. If vacancies reduce the Board below a quorum, the remaining directors may fill vacancies by a majority vote of those remaining, even if less than a quorum.
ARTICLE IVOfficers
§ 4.01 – Officers
The officers of the Association shall be a President, Vice President, Secretary, and Treasurer, each elected annually by the Board from among its members at the first Board meeting following the annual election. Officers may be removed from their officer positions at any time by majority vote of the Board.
§ 4.02 – Duties of Officers
  • President: Presides over all Board and Member meetings; executes contracts and official documents; serves as the Association's primary representative.
  • Vice President: Performs the duties of the President in the President's absence or incapacity; oversees special committees.
  • Secretary: Records and maintains minutes of all meetings; certifies documents; manages official correspondence and records.
  • Treasurer: Oversees financial accounts; prepares financial reports; ensures proper accounting practices; chairs the Finance Committee.
ARTICLE VMeetings of Members
§ 5.01 – Annual Meeting
The Annual Meeting of Members shall be held each year in January at a time and place designated by the Board. The primary purposes of the Annual Meeting are to elect directors, present financial reports, and conduct any other business properly brought before the Members.
§ 5.02 – Special Meetings
Special meetings of Members may be called by the President, by a majority vote of the Board, or by written petition signed by at least twenty percent (20%) of the Members in good standing. Special meetings shall be held within forty-five (45) days of the call or petition.
§ 5.03 – Notice Requirements
Written notice of all Member meetings shall be given to each Member not less than ten (10) nor more than sixty (60) days before the date of the meeting. Notice shall state the time, place, and purpose of the meeting and shall be delivered by first-class mail, electronic mail, or posted on the Association's official digital platform.
§ 5.04 – Quorum
At any meeting of Members, a quorum shall consist of Members holding twenty-five percent (25%) of the total votes either in person or by proxy. If a quorum is not present, the meeting may be adjourned to a later date, and the quorum requirement for the adjourned meeting shall be reduced to ten percent (10%).
ARTICLE VIAssessments and Dues
§ 6.01 – Regular Assessments
The Board shall establish the annual regular assessment by October 31st of each year for the following calendar year. Regular assessments are due on the first day of each quarter. The Board may increase regular assessments up to fifteen percent (15%) over the prior year without Member approval.
§ 6.02 – Special Assessments
The Board may levy special assessments for major capital improvements or emergency expenditures. Special assessments exceeding $1,000 per unit in any fiscal year require approval of a majority of the total membership by written ballot or meeting vote.
§ 6.03 – Late Fees and Interest
Assessments not paid within fifteen (15) days of the due date shall be subject to a late fee of $50. Unpaid balances shall accrue interest at the rate of 12% per annum from the due date. The Association may also recover reasonable attorney's fees and collection costs.
§ 6.04 – Liens
Unpaid assessments shall constitute a lien upon the unit. The Association may enforce this lien by foreclosure after an assessment has been delinquent for more than ninety (90) days, subject to applicable State law requirements, including required pre-lien notices.
ARTICLE VIIArchitectural Control
§ 7.01 – Architectural Review Committee
The Board shall appoint a three-member Architectural Review Committee (ARC) to review and approve or deny requests for modifications to the exterior of units or common areas. At least one (1) ARC member shall be a Board director.
§ 7.02 – Review Process
All proposed exterior modifications, alterations, or additions require prior written approval from the ARC. The ARC shall render its decision within thirty (30) days of receiving a complete application. Failure to respond within thirty (30) days constitutes conditional approval subject to compliance with all standards.
§ 7.03 – Appeals
A Member who is denied approval by the ARC may appeal to the full Board within fifteen (15) days of the denial. The Board shall hear the appeal at its next regular meeting and issue a final written decision within five (5) business days thereafter.
ARTICLE VIIIEnforcement and Dispute Resolution
§ 8.01 – Violations
Upon a determination that a Member or resident has violated any provision of the governing documents, the Association shall provide written notice of the alleged violation and an opportunity to cure. If the violation is not cured within the specified time, the Board may impose fines as set forth in the Fine Schedule adopted by the Board.
§ 8.02 – Fine Schedule
  • First violation notice: Written warning, no fine
  • Second violation (same issue): $100 fine
  • Third and subsequent violations: $200 per occurrence
  • Continuing violations: $50 per day after 10-day cure period
  • Serious/safety violations: Up to $500 at Board discretion
§ 8.03 – Hearing Rights
Members subject to fines have the right to a hearing before the Board. Hearing requests must be submitted in writing within fifteen (15) days of the fine notice. The Board shall schedule the hearing within thirty (30) days of the request and provide a written decision within five (5) business days.
§ 8.04 – Mediation
Prior to initiating legal action (other than lien enforcement or collection), the parties shall participate in good-faith mediation through a mutually agreed-upon mediator. Mediation costs shall be shared equally unless otherwise ordered by the mediator.
ARTICLE IXIndemnification and Insurance
§ 9.01 – Indemnification
The Association shall indemnify and hold harmless each director, officer, and committee member from claims, liabilities, and expenses arising out of their service on behalf of the Association, except in cases of willful misconduct, gross negligence, or bad faith. This indemnification shall include reasonable attorney's fees.
§ 9.02 – Required Insurance
  • Commercial General Liability: Minimum $2,000,000 per occurrence, $5,000,000 aggregate
  • Property Insurance: Full replacement cost value for all insurable Common Area structures
  • Directors & Officers (D&O): Minimum $1,000,000 per occurrence
  • Workers' Compensation: As required by State law for Association employees
  • Fidelity Bond: Minimum $500,000 covering all persons handling Association funds
ARTICLE XAmendments
§ 10.01 – Amendment Procedure
These Bylaws may be amended by an affirmative vote of sixty-seven percent (67%) of the total membership voting at a duly noticed meeting at which a quorum is present, or by a sixty-seven percent (67%) written ballot of all Members. Proposed amendments must be included in the meeting notice or ballot materials sent to all Members.
§ 10.02 – Board Authority to Amend
The Board may adopt minor amendments and corrections to these Bylaws (to resolve typographical errors, correct legal citations, or conform to applicable law) by unanimous vote of the entire Board, without Member approval. Such amendments shall be reported to Members at the next Annual Meeting.
§ 10.03 – Effective Date
All amendments shall become effective upon adoption and shall be filed with the appropriate governmental authority within thirty (30) days if required by applicable law. Copies of all amendments shall be provided to all Members within sixty (60) days of adoption.
