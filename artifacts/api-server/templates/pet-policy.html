🐾 This Pet Policy Addendum supplements the Community Rules & Regulations. All pet owners must complete the Pet Registration Form and receive written approval before their pet takes up residence in Lakeview Commons. This policy applies to all owners, tenants, and long-term guests.
📝
Pet Registration Requirements
Section 1
Mandatory Registration
All pets residing in Lakeview Commons must be registered with the management office within fourteen (14) calendar days of either (a) the Owner's or tenant's move-in date, or (b) the pet's acquisition date, whichever is applicable. Unregistered pets are a violation subject to fine.
Section 2
Registration Documentation Required
The following documents must be submitted with every pet registration:
  • Completed Pet Registration Form (available in the app or management office)
  • Current rabies vaccination certificate (dogs and cats)
  • Current DHPP or FVRCP vaccination documentation
  • Proof of spay or neuter (if applicable; intact animals require Board approval)
  • Current county pet license (dogs)
  • Color photograph of the pet
  • For restricted breeds: minimum $100,000 liability insurance policy showing Association as additional insured
Section 3
Annual Renewal
Pet registrations must be renewed annually by January 31st. Renewal requires updated vaccination documentation. An annual pet registration fee of $50 per pet is due at time of renewal. Late renewals (after February 28th) incur an additional $25 administrative fee.
Section 4
Maximum Number of Pets
Each unit may house a maximum of two (2) pets (dogs and cats combined). Reptiles, rodents, birds, and fish kept in tanks under 30 gallons do not count toward this limit but must still be disclosed. Exceptions to the two-pet limit require a written variance request and Board approval.

📋
Pet Conduct Rules
Section 5
Leash Requirement
All dogs must be kept on a leash not exceeding six (6) feet in length whenever outside the unit, including all common areas, parking areas, walkways, and grass areas. Retractable leashes longer than six feet in the extended position are prohibited. Owners must maintain physical control of the dog at all times.
Section 6
Waste Cleanup
Pet owners are required to immediately pick up and properly dispose of all pet waste in the designated pet waste stations located at each parking lot entrance and at six locations along the community walking path. Failure to clean up pet waste is subject to an immediate $50 fine. Repeat violations escalate as follows:
First offense$50
Second offense (same 12 months)$100
Third offense (same 12 months)$200 + warning letter
Fourth offense$300 + pet privilege review
Section 7
Prohibited Areas
Pets are not permitted in the following areas under any circumstances:
  • The pool area and within the pool barrier fence
  • The fitness center
  • Building lobbies and common indoor areas (except for transit through hallways on leash)
  • The playground and tot lot area
  • Designated no-pet landscaping zones (marked with signage)
Exception: Certified service animals are permitted in all areas in accordance with ADA and Fair Housing Act requirements.
Section 8
Noise and Nuisance
Owners are responsible for preventing their pets from creating persistent or unreasonable noise. Barking or other persistent animal vocalizations lasting more than fifteen (15) continuous minutes, or occurring intermittently for more than thirty (30) minutes within any one-hour period, constitute a noise violation. Owners who receive three (3) verified noise complaints within a twelve-month period may have their pet privileges reviewed by the Board.
Section 9
Bites and Aggressive Behavior
Any pet bite or aggressive incident resulting in injury to a person or another animal must be reported to the management office within 24 hours. The Board may require the pet to be permanently removed from the community following a bite incident or documented aggression, regardless of prior behavior history. All quarantine requirements under local law apply.
Board Adoption
This Pet Policy Addendum was adopted by the Board of Directors of Beyond HOA at Lakeview Commons at a regular Board meeting on May 15, 2023, by unanimous vote of 4–0. It supersedes the Pet Policy adopted February 2020 in its entirety.
