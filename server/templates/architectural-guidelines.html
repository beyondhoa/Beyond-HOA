What Requires ARC Approval
Any alteration, addition, or improvement to the exterior of a unit or any portion of the property visible from common areas or neighboring units requires prior written approval from the Architectural Review Committee.

Application Process
1
Complete the Application
Download and complete the Architectural Review Request Form (available in the Documents section of the Beyond HOA app or from the management office). Provide detailed description of proposed work, materials, colors, and dimensions.
2
Submit Supporting Documents
Include site plan or sketch showing location and dimensions; samples or photos of materials and colors; contractor name and license number (if applicable); and any required municipal permits (if applicable). Incomplete applications will be returned without review.
3
ARC Review
The ARC will review your application within thirty (30) calendar days of receiving a complete application. The ARC may request additional information, schedule a site visit, or conditionally approve an application with modifications.
4
Receive Written Decision
You will receive a written decision via email. Approval letters include any conditions that must be met. Work must commence within ninety (90) days of approval and be completed within the timeframe specified in the approval letter (typically 90 days for minor work, 180 days for major projects).
5
Final Inspection
Upon completion, notify the ARC in writing for a final inspection. The ARC will inspect the completed work to confirm compliance with the approved plans within fifteen (15) days of notification. Non-compliant work must be corrected within thirty (30) days or be subject to restoration orders.

Design Standards
Exterior Colors
Exterior paint colors must harmonize with the community's established color palette. The ARC-approved color families are earth tones (warm beiges, taupes, sage greens), coastal neutrals (soft grays, slate blues, off-whites), and traditional brick complement colors. Bright or primary colors, fluorescent finishes, and high-gloss paint on siding are not approved. The ARC maintains a binder of approved colors available for inspection at the management office.
Fencing
Permitted fence materials include cedar, redwood, pressure-treated pine (painted or stained), vinyl in earth tones, and black powder-coated aluminum. Maximum height is 6 feet in rear yards and 4 feet in side yards visible from the street. Chain-link, barbed wire, and corrugated metal fencing are prohibited.
Decks and Patios
Decks must be constructed of approved materials: natural wood (sealed or stained), composite decking in earth tones, or stamped/brushed concrete for patios. All decks must include proper drainage and conform to applicable building codes. Decks over 30 inches above grade require engineered plans and a municipal building permit, which must be submitted with the ARC application.
Solar Panels
Solar panels are permitted on rear roof slopes not visible from the street or must be architecturally integrated. Panels must not extend beyond the roofline. Wiring and conduit must be concealed or painted to match exterior. ARC approval required but cannot be unreasonably withheld for solar installations under State solar access laws.
Doors and Windows
Replacement windows must match or improve the style and proportion of original windows. Reflective, tinted, or painted glass is prohibited. Entry doors may be painted in accent colors within the approved palette (burgundy, forest green, navy, charcoal, and classic black are pre-approved accent door colors). Storm doors must be full-view style in the appropriate finish for the door color.

Appeals Process
Grounds for Appeal
Applicants who receive a denial or conditional approval from the ARC may appeal to the full Board of Directors within fifteen (15) calendar days of receiving the written decision. Appeals must be in writing and state specifically the grounds for the appeal and any new information not previously considered.
Appeal Hearing
The Board shall hear the appeal at the next regularly scheduled Board meeting following receipt of the appeal, provided the appeal is received at least seven (7) days before the meeting. The applicant may present their case for up to ten (10) minutes. The Board's decision shall be final and rendered in writing within five (5) business days of the hearing.
