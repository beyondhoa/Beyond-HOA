📊 Reports
President — Patricia Chen
President Chen reported that the community pool replastering project is underway and on track for a November 1st completion date. She highlighted a 6% decrease in common area maintenance costs year-over-year and thanked residents for complying with updated landscaping guidelines. The board is working with legal counsel to finalize updated parking enforcement procedures.
Treasurer — Sandra Torres
Year-to-date dues collection stands at 92.4%. Reserve fund balance is $1,793,200. Q3 operating expenses are on track, with utility savings of approximately $8,400 attributed to the solar panel installation on Building A completed in Q2. The preliminary 2026 budget draft will be circulated to board members by October 15th.
Management — Lisa Hartman
Management received 23 work orders in Q3, of which 21 have been resolved. Two complex HVAC repairs in Building C are pending parts delivery (ETA October 5th). The new landscape contractor (GreenWave Services) began service August 1st with positive resident feedback. Annual fire alarm testing is scheduled for October 22–23.
Landscaping Committee
The fall planting schedule has been finalized, with new drought-resistant plantings replacing the north-side flower beds. Committee recommends approving a $4,200 supplemental budget for irrigation system repairs identified during the transition to the new contractor.
🔄 Old Business
1. Pool Replastering — Progress Update
Contractor Aqua Blue Restoration confirmed the project is 60% complete. Pool is expected to reopen November 1st, 2025. Budget remains on track at $42,800 of the $45,000 approved budget.
2. Pet Registration Addendum — Final Review
The updated pet policy addendum was circulated for final board review. Minor language changes were incorporated. The addendum will take effect October 1, 2025. All residents with pets must re-register within 60 days.
Motion to adopt the updated Pet Policy Addendum effective October 1, 2025.
Moved by: Patricia Chen
Seconded by: Sandra Torres
Vote: PASSED 3–0
3. Lobby Security Upgrade — Vendor Selection
Three camera system proposals were reviewed in Q2. The board approved SecureVision Systems at the Q2 meeting. Installation was completed August 12th. System is fully operational. Post-installation resident survey shows 91% satisfaction with the upgrade.
🆕 New Business
1. Building B Elevator Modernization — RFP Authorization
Tom Reyes of ABC Elevator Co. presented an assessment of the Building B elevator, originally installed in 2006. Estimated 2–3 year remaining useful life. The board authorized management to issue an RFP to at least 3 licensed elevator contractors. Responses due November 15th for Q4 board consideration.
Motion to authorize issuance of an RFP for Building B elevator modernization.
Moved by: Sandra Torres
Seconded by: Patricia Chen
Vote: PASSED 3–0
2. Irrigation Repair — Supplemental Budget Approval
The board approved a $4,200 supplemental budget for irrigation system repairs as recommended by the Landscaping Committee.
Motion to approve $4,200 supplemental budget for irrigation repairs.
Moved by: Patricia Chen
Seconded by: David Kim
Vote: PASSED 3–0
3. 2026 Budget Planning Process
Treasurer Torres presented the Q4 budget planning timeline. Department heads will submit budget requests by October 1st. Draft budget to board by October 15th. Board workshop scheduled for November 6th. Final adoption at Q4 December meeting.
🗣️ Owner Open Forum

Twelve residents attended (9 in-person, 3 via Zoom). The open forum ran approximately 18 minutes.

  • Dog waste stations: Unit 115 requested additional dog waste stations near the north walking path. Management will install two additional stations by October 1st.
  • EV charging stations: Multiple residents inquired about installing EV charging stations in the parking structure. Board acknowledged growing interest; feasibility study to be included in 2026 capital planning.
  • Guest parking enforcement: Concerns raised about chronic guest parking violations. Board confirmed the updated visitor parking policy is in development and will be finalized at the Q4 meeting.
  • Pool closure communication: Residents asked for earlier notification of the pool closure for replastering. Board committed to 30-day advance notice for future amenity closures.
