📊 Reports
President — Patricia Chen
President Chen reported a strong close to 2025, with all major capital projects completed on schedule. She thanked the community for their participation in the annual survey and noted that 83% of respondents rated community management as "Good" or "Excellent." The 2026 operating budget was presented for formal adoption.
Treasurer — Sandra Torres
Treasurer Torres reported year-to-date dues collection at 94.2%, with $42,300 in outstanding balances under active collections. Reserve fund balance stands at $1,847,500 across all categories, representing 76% overall funding per the 2024 reserve study. Q4 operating expenses came in 3.1% under budget due to mild weather reducing utility costs.
Management — Lisa Hartman
Ms. Hartman reported 18 open work orders at the start of the quarter, with 15 closed by month-end. The pool replastering project was completed November 14th and received positive feedback from residents. Holiday lighting installation was completed across all common areas. Two new vendor contracts (landscaping and elevator maintenance) were finalized at improved rates.
Architectural Committee
7 architectural review applications received in Q4; 6 approved, 1 pending additional documentation. The committee chair reminded residents that all exterior modifications require prior written approval per CC&Rs Section 4.2.
🔄 Old Business
1. Parking Lot Resurfacing — Status Update
The board reviewed final punch-list items for the Phase 2 parking lot resurfacing project completed in October. All items have been resolved to management's satisfaction. Final payment of $28,400 was approved in October. Project officially closed.
2. Elevator Modernization — Contract Award
Following the RFP process initiated in Q3, the board reviewed three bids for the Building B elevator modernization. ThyssenKrupp Elevator was selected at $184,200. Work is scheduled to begin February 2026 with a 10-week completion timeline. Residents will receive 30-day advance notice.
3. Visitor Parking Policy — Implementation
Updated visitor parking policy (effective April 1, 2026) was finalized. Visitor pass booklets will be distributed to all units in January. Digital pass system vendor selection is ongoing; decision expected at Q1 2026 meeting.
🆕 New Business
1. Adoption of 2026 Operating Budget
Treasurer Torres presented the proposed 2026 Operating Budget totaling $1,108,200 in expenses with $1,139,580 in projected revenue, resulting in a $31,380 surplus. Monthly assessment remains unchanged at $385/unit.
Motion to adopt the 2026 Operating Budget as presented.
Moved by: Sandra Torres
Seconded by: Marcus Williams
Vote: PASSED 4–0
2. Holiday Decoration Policy
Following resident feedback, the board approved updated holiday decoration guidelines extending the display period through January 15th and permitting LED string lights on balcony railings (previously restricted). Policy effective immediately.
Motion to approve updated holiday decoration guidelines.
Moved by: Patricia Chen
Seconded by: David Kim
Vote: PASSED 4–0
3. Board Officer Elections for 2026
Annual officer elections were conducted. All incumbents were unanimously re-elected: Patricia Chen (President), Marcus Williams (Vice President), Sandra Torres (Treasurer), David Kim (Secretary). Officers will serve through December 2026.
🗣️ Owner Open Forum

Fourteen residents were in attendance. The open forum session ran approximately 22 minutes. Topics raised included:

  • Gym hours: Several residents requested extended fitness center hours (currently 6 AM–10 PM). The board agreed to survey residents and revisit at the Q1 2026 meeting.
  • Package lockers: Unit 204 inquired about installing smart package lockers in the mail room. Management to obtain quotes for the Q1 meeting.
  • Speeding in the garage: Resident from Unit 318 raised concerns about vehicle speeds in the parking structure. Management will install additional speed bump signage and consult with the security vendor.
  • Pool temperature: Request to extend the heated pool season by two weeks in spring and fall. Board took under advisement; cost estimate to be presented in Q1.
