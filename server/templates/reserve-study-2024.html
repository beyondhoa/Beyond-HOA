Executive Summary

Beyond HOA at Lakeview Commons is in strong financial health relative to its reserve obligations. As of July 2024, the Association's reserves are approximately 92% funded, well above the recommended minimum threshold of 70% and approaching the target of 100% fully funded status.

The study identified 48 reserve components across 8 major categories. Of these, 6 components have remaining useful life of less than 5 years and represent the highest near-term funding priorities. The largest upcoming expenditures are elevator modernization (2026), exterior painting cycle (2027), and pool deck resurfacing (2028).

The current annual reserve contribution of $120,000 is recommended to increase to $132,000 in 2025 and $145,000 in 2026 to maintain fully-funded status through 2034 given scheduled major projects. No special assessment is projected to be required during the study period under the baseline funding scenario.

Funding Recommendations

Baseline Scenario (Recommended): Annual contribution of $132,000 in 2025, increasing by $13,000 per year through 2028, then stabilizing at approximately $170,000 per year. Under this scenario, the fund remains above 85% funded throughout the study period and no special assessments are required.

Threshold Funding Scenario: Annual contribution of $110,000 (flat). Under this scenario, the fund dips below 70% funded in 2026–2027 during the elevator modernization, requiring either a temporary special assessment of approximately $1,200 per unit or short-term borrowing. Not recommended.

Full-Funding Scenario: Annual contribution of $158,000 beginning in 2025. Under this scenario, the fund achieves 100% funded status by 2027 and remains above 95% throughout. Provides maximum protection against unexpected major expenditures.

Board Recommendation: The Board has adopted the Baseline Scenario for 2025–2026 with a commitment to revisit the study in 2027 following completion of the elevator modernization project. The annual reserve contribution will increase from $120,000 to $132,000 effective January 1, 2025.
